## 3. Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of data, depending on how you use the Service.

### 3.1 Account and Authentication Data

We collect data needed to create, authenticate, and secure your account, including:

- Email address.
- Password authentication data. We store a password hash, not your plaintext password.
- Access tokens, refresh tokens, token expiry data, and session state.
- Account identifiers generated by the Service.

### 3.2 Portfolio and Investment Data

We collect the portfolio and investment information you enter, import, or generate through the Service, including:

- Portfolio names.
- Cryptocurrency symbols, token identifiers, mint addresses, and price lookup identifiers.
- Investment amounts, purchase prices, fees, dates, platforms, external trade identifiers, and deletion status.
- Estimated current prices, portfolio values, allocations, profit/loss calculations, and historical portfolio snapshots.
- Portfolio owner, member, invite, role, and status data, including member email addresses and user IDs.

This data can reveal information about your financial interests and cryptocurrency activity. Do not add data to Portfold that you do not want processed by the Service or visible to authorized members of a shared portfolio.

### 3.3 Sync Connection Data

If you choose to connect external sync providers, we collect and process the data needed to operate those connections, including:

- Bitget API key, secret, and passphrase.
- Solana wallet address.
- Sync status, last sync time, sync item counts, and sync error messages.

Bitget credentials and sync payloads are encrypted at rest on the backend. For Solana sync, you provide only a wallet address; provider API access is managed by Portfold on the backend and is not requested from you. You should use Bitget API keys with the minimum permissions needed for portfolio tracking and avoid granting withdrawal or trading permissions unless strictly necessary for your own separate purposes outside Portfold.

### 3.4 Third-Party Market, Exchange, and Blockchain Data

When sync or valuation features are used, we may obtain or request data from third-party providers, including:

- Bitget account asset and trade data for connected Bitget accounts.
- Solana wallet asset, signature, and transaction data for connected wallet addresses.
- Market price data for cryptocurrency symbols and token identifiers.
- Historical SOL price data used to estimate Solana transaction cost basis.

### 3.5 Device, App, and Technical Data

We may process technical data necessary to operate and secure the Service, including:

- IP address, request metadata, timestamps, and server logs generated when your device communicates with our backend.
- User agent and similar client/network metadata sent by the app or browser.
- App version, platform, operating system, and diagnostic data that may be included in standard request or infrastructure logs.
- Mobile crash reports and diagnostics collected through Firebase Crashlytics when the app crashes, such as stack traces, app version/build, operating system version, device model, crash timestamp, installation identifier, and basic device state.
- Local device state needed to keep you signed in, select an active portfolio, and apply app lock settings.

As of the last updated date, the mobile app includes Firebase Crashlytics for crash reporting. It does not include advertising SDKs or third-party analytics SDKs. The Android app requests internet access. It does not request camera, microphone, contacts, precise location, photos, SMS, or call log permissions. The iOS app may use Face ID through the operating system to unlock a saved session; we do not receive or store your biometric templates.

### 3.6 Local Device Storage

The mobile app stores some data locally on your device to make the app work, including:

- A local database cache of portfolios, investments, prices, snapshots, allocations, and coin metadata.
- Authentication tokens and session settings in secure platform storage, such as Android encrypted preferences or iOS Keychain.
- Biometric/app-lock settings.

Android app backup and data extraction are disabled in the app configuration.

### 3.7 Data We Do Not Intentionally Collect

We do not intentionally collect:

- Payment card data. As of the effective date, Portfold does not offer paid billing, subscriptions, Stripe, PayPal, or similar payment integrations.
- Advertising identifiers for advertising or tracking.
- Precise geolocation, contacts, photos, microphone, camera, SMS, or call log data.
- Private keys or wallet seed phrases. You should never enter private keys or seed phrases into Portfold.

## 6. How We Share Data

We do not sell personal data. We do not share personal data for third-party advertising or cross-app tracking.

We may share data in the following situations.

### 6.1 Service Providers and Infrastructure

We may use infrastructure and operational providers, such as hosting, database, deployment, networking, logging/monitoring, email, crash reporting, or support providers where applicable, to run the Service. These providers process data on our behalf to operate infrastructure and provide support. For example, the mobile apps use Google Firebase Crashlytics for crash diagnostics.

### 6.2 External Sync and Market Data Providers

When you use sync or valuation features, relevant data may be sent to or requested from external providers, including:

- Bitget, for account assets, trade fills, and market price data.
- Helius/Solana RPC infrastructure, for wallet assets, signatures, transactions, and token price metadata.
- Jupiter price API, for token price data.
- Binance market data endpoints, for historical SOL price data.

For example, if you connect a Solana wallet, the wallet address and related request metadata may be processed by Solana RPC infrastructure. If you connect Bitget, Bitget receives API-authenticated requests from or on behalf of your account. These providers may process data under their own terms and privacy policies.

### 6.3 Shared Portfolio Members

If you create, join, invite users to, or participate in a shared portfolio, authorized portfolio members may see portfolio data according to their role. This can include portfolio names, owner/member emails, roles, investment holdings, valuation data, allocations, and history.

## 8. Security

We use technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal data, including:

- Password hashing.
- Token-based authentication.
- Encrypted storage for mobile session tokens on supported platforms.
- AES-GCM encryption at rest for backend sync payloads such as exchange credentials.
- Role-based portfolio access controls.
- HTTPS for production API traffic.
- Limited mobile permissions.

No system is perfectly secure. You are responsible for keeping your account password, device, API keys, and wallet information secure. Do not share credentials with unauthorized people, and do not enter private keys or seed phrases into the Service.

## 9. Retention

We retain personal data for as long as needed to provide the Service, maintain your account, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, and protect the Service.

In general:

- Account data is retained while your account remains active.
- Portfolio, investment, snapshot, allocation, member, invite, and sync data is retained while needed for the Service or until deleted or de-identified.
- Refresh tokens are retained while valid or until rotated, expired, replaced, or deleted.
- Deleted investments may be retained as soft-deleted records to preserve sync behavior and prevent re-importing previously removed external trades.
- Sync connections are deleted when you disconnect the provider, subject to backups and logs.
- Operational logs and backups may be retained for a limited period for security, reliability, and disaster recovery.

If you request account deletion, we will take reasonable steps to delete or de-identify personal data associated with your account, unless retention is required or permitted by law, security, fraud prevention, dispute resolution, backup integrity, or other legitimate reasons. You can request deletion by emailing `info@koral.solutions`.
